02/08 William Spectral sequences and deformations of homotopy theories I

One of the oldest problems in stable homotopy theory is simple: just compute the stable homotopy groups of spheres. This turns out to be difficult, and a complete answer may never be known, but the computations continue. A recent technique being applied to great success can be summarized as: don‚Äôt just compute the stable homotopy groups of the sphere spectrum, also compute the stable homotopy groups of other sphere spectra. There are a few methods for building these deformations; the goal of this sequence of two talks is to describe the concrete approach to this deformation story using filtered objects.
